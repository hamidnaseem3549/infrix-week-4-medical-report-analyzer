─────────────────────────────────────────────────
CLINICAL IMPRESSION
─────────────────────────────────────────────────
The laboratory findings are consistent with Iron Deficiency Anemia (IDA).
The microcytic, hypochromic anemia pattern (low MCV, MCH, MCHC) combined
with markedly reduced iron stores (low ferritin, low serum iron, elevated
TIBC) strongly supports this diagnosis.

Elevated WBC count with neutrophilia may indicate an ongoing infection or
inflammatory process and warrants clinical correlation.

Vitamin D deficiency noted — supplementation recommended.
Borderline Vitamin B12 — dietary review and follow-up advised.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────
RECOMMENDATIONS
─────────────────────────────────────────────────
1. Initiate oral iron supplementation (e.g., Ferrous Sulfate 325mg once daily)
2. Investigate the underlying cause of iron deficiency (dietary, GI blood loss)
3. Vitamin D3 supplementation (50,000 IU weekly for 8 weeks)
4. Dietary counseling — increase iron-rich foods (red meat, leafy greens, lentils)
5. Repeat CBC and iron studies after 8 weeks of treatment
6. Clinical correlation required for elevated WBC — rule out infection
7. Consider B12 supplementation or dietary modification
